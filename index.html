My Story

I'm currently looking for a full-time position as a Full-Stack Software Engineer. I specialize in the MEAN stack with a Backbone.js front-end. However, as a self-taught developer, I've realized that the mantra of "It's not what you know, it's what you can find out. " is what actually makes developers successful.

My story: I studied Philosophy at UCLA with the expectation of becoming an attorney. Despite scoring in the 90th percentile on the LSAT, I decided that attending law school wasn't the right choice for me. Reflecting on my studies, I realized that the classes I enjoyed the most in college included advanced symbolic logic and philosophy of language. Given the abundance of online resources in computer programming and the fact that I thought that the one computer science course I took years ago was fun -- despite being mostly about Excel and Access -- I decided that programming was a field worth exploring. Once I built my first project, I became hooked on two things: solving challenges and exploring possibilities.
